How to Run AI-Powered 1:1s That Actually Drive Results

Sep 12, 2025 · 7 min read

The one-on-one is the highest-leverage half hour on an engineering manager's calendar — and the easiest to wing. AI doesn't fix a bad 1:1, but it removes the friction that makes good 1:1s hard to sustain: prep, note-taking, and follow-through. Here's a structure you can adopt this week.

1. Prep in five minutes, not zero

Before each 1:1, pull the engineer's recent context — merged PRs, open tickets, last meeting's action items — and ask your AI assistant to surface themes and one question worth asking. The point isn't to script the conversation; it's to walk in informed instead of improvising.

2. Capture without breaking eye contact

Use transcription or a shared doc so you're present in the conversation rather than typing through it. Afterward, have AI distill the notes into decisions, commitments, and follow-ups — owned by name, with dates.

3. Close the loop

Most 1:1 value leaks between meetings. Turn each commitment into a tracked item and let your assistant remind you before the next session. The compounding effect of consistent follow-through is what builds trust.
