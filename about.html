About

Engineering leadership, rebuilt around AI.

Most engineering-management writing falls into one of two buckets: deeply technical posts aimed at individual contributors, or abstract leadership theory that never touches a real team. The space in between — practical guidance for managers who want to actually use AI in their day-to-day work — is mostly empty.

This blog lives in that gap. The premise is simple: AI is changing how engineering managers prepare for 1:1s, evaluate candidates, measure productivity, and make technical bets. The managers who treat AI as a genuine part of their workflow — not a novelty — will pull ahead. The goal here is to make that transition concrete.

What you can expect

  • Frameworks, not hot takes. Repeatable structures you can apply to your own team.
  • Honest tool reviews. What helps, what's demo-ware, and how to tell the difference.
  • No fabricated numbers. Claims are sourced or clearly marked as opinion.
This is a starter site. Replace this copy and the placeholder posts with your own voice and real examples — the structure is built to make that easy.
