Beyond DORA: AI-Driven Productivity Measurement

Sep 12, 2025 · 8 min read

DORA metrics — deployment frequency, lead time, change-failure rate, time to restore — remain a solid baseline. But when AI assistants write a meaningful share of the code, output-shaped measures get noisier. Here's how to think about productivity without fooling yourself.

Measure flow, not volume

More commits or more lines means little when AI can generate both cheaply. Track how smoothly work moves from idea to production: where it waits, where it bounces back.

Watch the review bottleneck

If AI speeds up authoring, review becomes the constraint. Code-review latency is often the most honest signal of whether AI is actually accelerating delivery.

Keep a human quality signal

Pair quantitative flow metrics with a qualitative read — team confidence, incident trends, rework rate — so speed gains don't quietly trade against reliability.

Ground this with your own dashboard and the metric definitions your org uses. Avoid borrowed benchmarks unless you can cite the source.
